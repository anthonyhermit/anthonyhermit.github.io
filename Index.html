About Judo

Judo, meaning "gentle way," is a Japanese martial art developed by Jigoro Kano in 1882. It emphasizes using an opponent's strength against them through throws, grappling, and submissions.

Philosophy

Judo promotes mutual benefit, respect, and personal growth.

Training

Learn techniques, improve fitness, and build mental resilience.

Judo Techniques

O-Goshi

A hip throw where the tori lifts the uke over their hip.

Seoi-Nage

A shoulder throw, one of Judo's original techniques.

Osoto-Gari

A leg sweep that unbalances the opponent backward.

History of Judo

Judo was founded by Jigoro Kano in 1882 in Japan. Combining elements of traditional Jujitsu, Kano created a system that focused on efficiency and safety. Judo became an Olympic sport in 1964 for men and 1992 for women, spreading its influence worldwide.

1882

Jigoro Kano founds the Kodokan, the first Judo school.

1964

Judo debuts at the Tokyo Olympics.

Today

Millions practice Judo in dojos across the globe.
